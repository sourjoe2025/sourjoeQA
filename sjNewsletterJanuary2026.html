Notes from the Test Kitchen: A Resurrection Story

We were cleaning out a refrigerator in December and stumbled across a jar of White Flour Starter. It was a gift from a good friend in the community, and in the past, it yielded amazing loaves. But life got busy.

The label said, "Last Fed: August 2024." It was now December 2025.

The jar was a wretched sight—full of dark, hooch-covered liquid. It looked abandoned, but sourdough is resilient. We decided to see if we could wake it up.

The Process:

  • We drained most (but not all) of the hooch.
  • We stirred the remaining liquid back in, then added fresh flour and warm water.
  • A vigorous stir to aerate, and then we waited.

After 10 hours? Not much movement. We discarded ¾ of the mix and fed it again (equal parts flour and warm water). After another 8 hours? Life.

It is now sweet-smelling, lively, and back in our rotation. This little experiment was a testament to the resilience of the all-powerful SCOBY (Symbiotic Community Of Bacteria and YEAST).

Moral of the Resurrection Story: If your starter has been neglected, don't throw it out—feed it!
